The Core Cycle Process

Four weeks to find the facts and build a routine.

1

Week 1 — Baseline

Following a successful DQ Check, we ingest your data against our Common Schema. We map SKUs and shifts to establish your true operational baseline.

2

Week 2 — Anomaly Map

Our deterministic rules engine scans for cash variance, void/comp clusters, and ghost hours. We identify the highest-priority issues.

3

Week 3 — Action Plan

We deliver your 1-page report with a maximum of three simple, high-impact actions. These are clear, behavioural routines for your team to adopt.

4

Week 4 — Verification

We re-run the analysis on the latest data to quantify the impact of the new routines and verify the fix. Next steps are agreed for your next cycle.

FAQ

Venue questions, straight answers

What is the Data Health Check?
The Health Check is our mandatory first step. You provide four weeks of POS and roster files (CSV/XLS only), and we produce a Data Quality (DQ) report. It tells you if your data is complete, consistent, and clean enough for a full audit. It’s a low-cost way to diagnose your data health before committing to a full cycle.
Why do I need a Health Check before a full audit?
Because “garbage in, garbage out.” A full audit is useless if your base data is unreliable. The Health Check de-risks the process for both of us. It ensures we can deliver meaningful, accurate insights during a Core Cycle and allows us to standardize our analysis, which keeps costs down.
Will this work with our POS?
Yes, as long as your system can export line-item sales, shifts, and Z-reads to CSV or XLSX format. We do not support scanned PDFs or photos of reports in our standard cycles.
What if our Data Health Check comes back with a “Fail”?
The DQ Report will list the specific blockers (e.g., missing files, inconsistent data, high percentage of null values). We can then provide a separate, one-time quote for data remediation or guide your team on how to fix the export process for future checks.
Is this a subscription?
No. Our model is based on discrete “Cycles.” The Standing Cycle gives you the benefits of a recurring service (a reserved slot and lower price) without the commitment of a subscription. You can pause or stop at any time.
